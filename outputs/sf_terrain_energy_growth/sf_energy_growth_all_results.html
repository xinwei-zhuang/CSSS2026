Minimal Rules

1

Building function

Every building is either residential or commercial. PV and batteries are assets a building can buy, not extra building functions.

2

Entry

The grid starts empty. Each day a small number of residential startups choose empty buildable cells with high solar/productivity opportunity.

3

Sharing

If a building has surplus, it can serve a requester through adjacent paid agreement edges. One transaction can add at most one adjacent agreement edge.

4

Hierarchy

Blocks are not predefined. A block is read afterward as a connected component of paid adjacent transmission agreements.

Interpretation

The first layer is individual building investment and local energy sharing. The second layer appears only when transmission agreements connect buildings into larger components. That means the hierarchy is not a block grid imposed in advance; it is an agreement graph that grows from energy and money constraints.

QuestionCan a minimal one-rule sharing system form higher-level transmission structure?
Current answerYes, in this run the final agreement graph has 3 connected components, with the largest containing 311 buildings. Commercial reaches 37 / 365 occupied cells, about 1:9.
CautionCommercial conversion is still rare in this parameter setting, so the land-use layer remains mostly residential.

Settings

Grid28 x 28 over San Francisco bounding box; 419 buildable terrain cells.
Simulation length365 days, hourly steps, seed 20250624.
Initial conditionDay 0 is an empty SF terrain grid. No building exists at the start.
Residential entryOne new residential startup enters per day, chooses an empty buildable cell by 0.55 solar opportunity + 0.45 productivity opportunity + small noise, and receives about $10,000 startup funding.
Building functionsOnly residential and commercial. Commercial can only emerge by upgrading an existing residential building after money/service/productivity conditions make payback short enough.
Commercial pressureCommercial conversion is capped by a 10% market-share target, so final type mix approaches roughly 1 commercial per 9 residential buildings without preassigning commercial zones.
Productivity scalingResidential income uses a smaller productivity scale (0.45); commercial income uses a larger productivity scale (1.25).
TransmissionEnergy moves along adjacent paid agreement edges; efficiency per edge = 0.965; transit fee = $0.012/kWh per transit node.
CostsPV $2,410/kW; battery $4,660 per 10 kWh; cable/agreement $650; floor $6,000; commercial upgrade $3,600.
O&MPV $0.10/kW/day; battery $0.015/kWh/day; cable $0.04/link/day.

Data Sources And Assumptions

ClimateNASA POWER hourly ALLSKY_SFC_SW_DWN and T2M for 2025 at a San Francisco point.
Solar spatial factorWest-to-east fog gradient plus small south-facing aspect adjustment.
TerrainSynthetic SF hills fallback. Height/elevation is shown as context; productivity is mapped from local slope, not elevation.
Load profilesOne residential and one commercial shape sampled from local cleaned hourly profiles.
Load calibrationResidential shape scaled to 6,200 kWh/year; commercial shape scaled to 82,000 kWh/year.
Hierarchy definitionConnected components of paid adjacent transmission agreements. No predefined block grid, no block treasury.
