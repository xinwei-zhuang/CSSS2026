Rules

1
Initial conditionDay 0 is empty buildable SF terrain. No building exists before startup entry.
2
Entry and investmentOne residential startup enters each day by choosing high solar/productivity opportunity. PV, batteries, cables, floors, and commercial upgrades all cost money.
3
SharingGenerosity = 1 only. A surplus building gives energy to the first requester reachable through adjacent paid agreement edges.
4
EmergenceNo extra building functions are defined. The second layer is read only from connected components of paid agreement edges.

Data And Parameters

ClimateNASA POWER hourly ALLSKY_SFC_SW_DWN, 2025, San Francisco point; then west-to-east fog gradient and aspect adjustment.
TerrainSynthetic SF hills fallback. USGS EPQS point queries were tested but too slow for 28 x 28 direct sampling.
LoadOne residential and one commercial profile sampled from local cleaned hourly profiles, then scaled to 6,200 and 82,000 kWh/year.
EntryOne residential startup per day; site score = 0.55 solar opportunity + 0.45 productivity opportunity + small noise.
CommercialResidential buildings can upgrade when productivity/service/money make commercial payback short enough; conversion is capped near 10% market share.
ProductivityResidential productivity scale = 0.45; commercial productivity scale = 1.25.
CostsPV $2.41/Wdc; battery $466/kWh x 10 kWh unit; cable $650/link; floor $6,000; commercial upgrade $3,600.
